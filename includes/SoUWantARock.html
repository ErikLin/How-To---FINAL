1

Choose your rock

There are many rocks in the world, but your rock should be special and unique to better fit your personality. These are some qualities you may want to take into consideration

  • Texture. Is it smooth? or soft?
  • Color. Some rocks are grey, while others are dark grey.
  • Size. The bigger the better I always say
  • Personality. It's important that you get along with your rock, you'll be spending a lot of time together after all

2

Don't forget to bathe your rock

No one likes a smelly rock! It's important that you keep your rock squeaky clean. Rocks don't have much of an imune system, so keeping them clean is essential to their survival.

3

Dress your rock

OH THE INDESCENCY! *SCREAMS* THINK OF THE CHILDREN! You wouldn't step outside without any clothes so why would your rock? Each rock has its own unique sense of style and you should respect that. Have a talk with your rock to decide which outfits are best for it.

4

Choose a place to put your rock

you'll want to give it a view. Rocks are used to exciting views like riverbeds, mountain tops, and your neighborhood park. Ideally youll want to place your rock by a window, or in a lively part of the house. Be sure your rock is out of reach from other pets. They are highly territorial.

5

Make sure you give your rock some love

Talk to your rock. Grab lunch together. No one likes sitting alone in the corner. You got this rock for companionship, not just for something to look at. Wold rocks are often surounded by frineds and family, so keeping them stimulated with conversation is essential to their sanity.

6

Schedual a play date for your rock

Rocks are very social creatures. You'll be a great friend, but its nice to play with someone of your own species once in a while. Find a friend with a rock to hang out with, or make a new friend with our Rock Pall finder
